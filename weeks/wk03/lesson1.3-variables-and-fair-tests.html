📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Two students each grow a bean plant. One waters daily in a sunny window; the other waters twice a week in a dim closet. The sunny plant grows taller. What caused that?
There's no way to tell from this setup — too many things changed at once. That's the single most common flaw in a "backyard experiment," and today's lesson is about exactly how to prevent it.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to identify independent and dependent variables in an experiment, and explain why controlling variables is what separates an experiment from any other kind of investigation.

Your science notebook
📚 PART 1 · THREE KINDS OF VARIABLES

In a real experiment, you change exactly one thing on purpose — the independent variable — and hold everything else the same, so that any change you measure in the dependent variable can be attributed to that one deliberate change. Everything you deliberately keep the same is a controlled variable. This is not busywork. It is the entire logical structure that lets a result mean anything at all.

⚡ TAP TO REVEAL
Independent variable
The one thing YOU deliberately change on purpose in an experiment.
⚡ TAP TO REVEAL
Dependent variable
The result you measure, which depends on (responds to) the independent variable.
⚡ TAP TO REVEAL
Controlled variable
Anything you deliberately keep the SAME across every trial, so it cannot explain your result.
📚 PART 2 · NOT EVERY QUESTION GETS AN EXPERIMENT

Not every scientific question can be answered this way. A geologist studying how the Grand Canyon formed cannot run a controlled experiment on an actual canyon. Instead, geology (like much of astronomy) relies on systematic observation: careful, repeated, structured observation of something you cannot manipulate. Both experiments and systematic observations produce real scientific knowledge; they are just different tools for different kinds of questions.

⚡ TAP TO REVEAL
Repetition
Running YOUR OWN trial multiple times to make sure your result wasn't a fluke.
⚡ TAP TO REVEAL
Replication
SOMEONE ELSE, in a different place, with their own materials, getting the same result you did.
⚠️ WATCH OUT
A finding that only you have ever produced is much weaker than a finding the whole scientific community has replicated. This is why a single dramatic headline about "a new study" should always be read with a little healthy skepticism until other scientists have checked it.
⚡ TRY IT · EXPERIMENT OR SYSTEMATIC OBSERVATION?
For each investigation, decide which tool it actually is.
1. A geologist records and compares rock layers exposed in canyon walls across a region.

💪 ONE CHANGE AT A TIME

Change one thing on purpose. Hold everything else steady. That single habit is what turns a backyard guess into real evidence — and it's the habit you'll use in every lab this year.
